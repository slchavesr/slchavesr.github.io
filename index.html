I am currently a full time PhD student at the University of Western Ontario, under the supervision of Prof. Matthias Franz. My main area of research are the syzygies in equivariant cohomology. Among my mathematical interests are algebraic topology, transformation groups, symplectic geometry and homological algebra. During Winter 2020 I am a Long Term Visitor in the Thematic Program on Toric Topology at the Fields Inistute in Toronto. I am passionate about teaching mathematics as well, and in my spare time I learn some coding and data science, play guitar and some videogames.

I am currently (Fall 2019) organizing the Mathematics Graduate Student Seminar at Western. Visit the website to get more info about the seminar. I participated in Brainhack Western 2019 relating mathematics and neuroscience; wining an innovative award for our proposed project on How does the brain sound.
